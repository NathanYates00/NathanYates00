Insuring your car

Main types of car insurance

Third party insurance

Third party is the bare minimum requirement legally, which covers yourself for damage to another person's vehicle, property or injury in an accident. Although it covers the other persons car, it does not cover the damages of your own.

Third party, fire and theft insurance

Identical to third party, it covers the damage of the other person's vehicle, property or injury, but also covers your own car for loss or damage via theft or fire.

Comprehensive insurance

Lastly is comprehensive, which includes third party, fire and theft insurance, as well as cover for stolen belongings within your car, medical or legal expenses, hiring a replacement vehicle and also covers death or injury to yourself or a family member.

Which insurance type should I go for?

Obviously with the third party insurance being the cheapest option due to it only covering the other person within the accident, some may opt for the third party option.

On the other hand, fire and theft can be common nowadays, and dependant on your area where you live where theft could be a problem, maybe you would prefer to opt for fire and theft insurance on top of third party.

Comprehensive is the most expensive, and may be more enticing once you gain a few years of no-claims before adding it to your policy. Comprehensive does cover you entirely though, which is the peace of mind option that some may prefer.

What is no-claims?

No-claims is added each year you do not make a claim, which is beneficial as this then brings down the cost of your policy. Usually this caps at about 10 years which will then display as 10+ years.

Due to having zero no-claims when you are a new driver, insurance costs will be at the highest point, which does decide which car you can have. Typically a car that is less than £1000 to buy will still be over £1000 a year on insurance as you are more likely to have an accident compared to someone that is experienced.
