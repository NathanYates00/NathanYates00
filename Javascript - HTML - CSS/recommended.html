Cars right for you

Skoda Citigo

The Skoda Citigo is one of the four recommended cars on this website, due to it being a great first car and not bad looking too. The specifications of the car are favourable for new drivers and get the job done nicely.

The author of the website has had a new Citigo in the past and it served well for almost three years, had no problems and was great for short and long journeys.

Specifications

With a very high fuel economy of around 50 miles per gallon (MPG), the Citigo does extremely well on fuel for a petrol engine. It also has 60 Brake Horse Power (BHP).
Being in insurance group 1, the Citigo's car insurance will be one of the lowest cost policies around, a great factor for new drivers.
Additionally, the road tax is only £20 a year, compared to some that are way over £100 a year.

Dacia Sandero

The Dacia Sandero has been poor in the past, but with the new model, the Sandero is one to consider.
A great car that is very cheap for what you get, with a price of around £8000 new.

Due to the new model being so good, it's the 2021 winner of the "Best car for under £10,000". You'll be seeing more of these Sanderos around with their reputation being high currently.

Specifications

Similar to the Citigo, the Sandero has a very high fuel economy of around 50 MPG too, doing very well on a full tank of fuel. Having 90 BHP as well.
The insurance group is slightly higher being around 8, but will still be a low cost policy which is very favourable.
Identical to the Citigo, the road tax is £20 a year.

Kia Picanto

Next is the Picanto, a great to drive car which is both well made and spacious. The exterior is nice looking and the interior looks more expensive than what it is.
A lot more popular in recent years, especially for new drivers.

Although it is a small car, majority in this list are small. But this feature helps with being in cities as large vehicles can cause a headache if inexperienced with city driving.

Specifications

You do find many of these small city cars to have great fuel economy, and this is no different being around 55 MPG. Also having 70 BHP.
Like the Citigo, it is in the lowest insurance group of 1, so those insurance policies will be as low as possible, great for someone with zero no-claims.

Seat Ibiza

The Ibiza has been great for years, and continues to be purchased by both new and more experienced drivers. A more larger car than the other three listed above, and similar to a Ford Fiesta or the Vauxhall Corsa.

The exterior and interior have smart styling, additionally coming with lots of in-car technology. It is very affordable for the product you get, and does not cost much to run either.

Specifications

The Ibiza has a fuel economy of around 45 MPG, which is lower than the other cars listed but still a high MPG to have. It also comes with 80 BHP.
It is in insurance group 2, so the insurance policy is not going to be a high price, and will decrease as you gain your no-claims bonus.
The road tax is £150, an additional £130 per year compared to the cars listed.
